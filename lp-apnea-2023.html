How We Can Help You Get Restful Sleep

Snoring

Snoring is an issue for millions of people no matter their gender or age. It is not only a problem for the one who snores but can be very disruptive for the bed partner.  Snoring can interrupt the sleep cycle and leave you both feeling tired and unrefreshed no matter how many hours of sleep you get. Plus, untreated snoring can lead to sleep apnea, which is a potentially life-threatening condition.  

Sleep Apnea

Sleep apnea is a potentially life-threatening disorder that causes deficient amounts of oxygen to the heart, brain, and lungs. This condition happens due to a collapse of the airway only while we sleep. Most people with moderate-heavy snoring will test positive for sleep apnea.  As with snoring, sleep apnea can also be an issue with your bed partner, leaving you both unrefreshed, tired, and foggy-headed. Untreated sleep apnea can lead to an array of overall health issues, including diabetes, strokes, heart attacks, and even death.

CPAP Therapy

Wearing a CPAP is a common treatment for sleep apnea. If you love your CPAP, we highly recommend you stay with it.  However, many people have difficulty with this treatment for reasons like ill-fitting masks, claustrophobia, hassle, and discomfort. These people are great candidates for oral appliance therapy.

Oral Appliance Therapy

Snoring and sleep apnea are both successfully treated with custom-fitted mouthpieces (oral appliances).  Our oral appliances fit on both the top and bottom teeth, designed to be worn only while sleeping. The mouthpiece clears the airway to allow oxygen to the heart, brain, and lungs. This opens the airway, eliminating snoring while also allowing for deeper, more restful sleep.

Testing

While sleep apnea and snoring have very similar symptoms, only a sleep test can differentiate the two.  Both conditions can you leave you unrefreshed, tired, and forgetful. However, only sleep apnea is directly related to heart attacks, strokes, and death.  Testing can usually be done in the comfort of your own bed with a home sleep test, and your results are usually back within a couple of hours. However, a PSG (in lab test) may be preferred depending on your case.
